# Product Brief

## Working Name

Community Survival Platform

## Product Statement

Build a community-operated survival platform inspired by the charm, exploration, and player-built creativity of Valheim, but designed from the start for:

- 100+ player communities
- Discord-native operations
- event-driven progression
- first-class extension and plugin support
- operator tooling that reduces volunteer overhead

## Who It Serves

Primary users:
- players in long-lived survival communities
- community owners and guild leaders
- volunteer moderators and event hosts
- modders and content creators

## Core Promise

The platform should let communities focus on memories, events, and creativity instead of fragile mod stacks, manual bookkeeping, and undocumented server behaviors.

## MVP Promise

The first milestone is not "replace all of Valheim."

The first milestone is:
- a persistent shared zone
- safe and readable travel
- visible distant player-built settlements
- authoritative progression and guild state
- Discord-linked roles and events
- admin tooling that explains why progress changed

## Non-Goals

Not in the first release:
- full biome parity with Valheim
- total content parity with the source game
- complete custom engine development
- community-owned combat authority
- unbounded plugin access to private internals

## Product Pillars

1. Player creativity is world art.
2. Travel should be readable before it is dangerous.
3. Distant presence should be cheap and meaningful.
4. Scale must come from relevance and partitioning, not brute force.
5. Community content should be data-driven first, code-driven second.
6. Volunteers should spend time creating experiences, not chasing undocumented state.

## First Playable Scenario

A player joins a server-linked Discord community, enters one persistent region, follows a visible road to a player-built settlement, contributes to a guild challenge, earns rank progress from authoritative events, and sees the resulting change reflected in both the game and the admin tools.
